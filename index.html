I. The Approach

Most cross-border deals fail not in the term sheet, but in the room afterwards — where culture, hierarchy, and unspoken expectation quietly reshape every commitment.

语

Fluent in both rooms.

Mandarin at the level required for a board presentation in Shanghai. Serbian and English at the level required for a regulatory hearing in Brussels. Translation is the floor — interpretation of intent, hierarchy, and face is the work.

理

Management that travels.

Operational leadership shaped across multinational structures, joint ventures, and stakeholder ecosystems where Western quarterly thinking meets Eastern long-horizon planning. The discipline to align both without flattening either.

桥

A bridge with load-bearing walls.

Not a translator. Not a fixer. A consigliere for the principals on both sides — present in negotiation, persistent through execution, accountable for the outcome long after the contract is signed and the press release is filed.
